About Me

Hi! I'm Christine Mari, a Japanese-American comic artist and writer. My work has been featured in The New Yorker, The Washington Post, and Elle Magazine. I was born in Tokyo, Japan and am currently based in Los Angeles.

My debut graphic memoir was released in Fall 2024 by Little Brown, Ink (Hachette Book Group). Through my autobiographical comics, I explore themes of mental health, mixed-race identity, and the profound beauty found in everyday moments.

My artistic journey began when I was fifteen with my first book, "Diary of a Tokyo Teen," an illustrated travelogue about one summer in Japan. This work evolved into my ongoing series of online comics that resonate with readers who find comfort in honest, gentle storytelling.

I love creating simple black-and-white illustrations that speak to universal experiences of anxiety, belonging, and self-discovery. When I'm not drawing, you can find me enjoying rainy days and grocery store birthday cake.
